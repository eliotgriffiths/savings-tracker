AI Import — Privacy

⚠ Before using AI import, please be aware:

When you import a file, its contents — including transaction amounts, dates, and merchant names — are sent to the AI provider you have selected. This data leaves your device and is processed on that provider's servers.

If you have Google Drive backup connected without encryption enabled, your API key is also stored in your backup file. Anyone with access to that file could read your key.

Vault does not store or transmit your files or API keys to its own servers. Your original files are never modified or deleted.

Provider privacy policies:

Each provider has its own data handling practices. Please review their privacy policy before use, as policies may change over time.

01
Who we are

Vault is a personal savings tracker available at vaultsavings.app. It is an independent application with no corporate entity behind it.

02
What data Vault collects

Vault collects no personal data. The app has no backend server, no database, and no analytics infrastructure. There is no mechanism by which your data reaches us.

Everything you enter — transactions, amounts, dates, and settings — is stored exclusively in your browser's local storage on your own device. It never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to connect a backup.

In plain terms: We cannot see your data. We do not have it and we never receive it. If you cleared your browser storage right now, your Vault data would be permanently gone — there is no copy we could restore from.
03
Google Drive backup

Vault offers an optional Google Drive backup. Connecting Google Drive is not required to use Vault. If you do choose to connect it, the following applies:

Scope of access: Vault requests only the drive.file permission — the most restricted scope available. This grants Vault access solely to files that Vault itself created. Vault cannot read, modify, or access any other file in your Google Drive.

What is stored: A single file named vault-savings-backup.json containing your transaction data. If you have enabled encryption, this file is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before being written to Drive, so the contents are protected even within your own Drive.

What Vault does not access: Vault does not request access to your Google profile, email address, contacts, calendar, or any other Google service.

Your Drive file is yours. You can view, download, or delete it at any time directly from Google Drive. Disconnecting Drive within Vault stops future syncing but does not delete the file.
04
Local file and network backup

Vault also supports saving to a local file on your device or to a network location such as a NAS or WebDAV server. These backups are written directly from your browser to your chosen destination. No data passes through any server we operate.

05
Cookies and tracking

Vault uses no cookies, no analytics platforms, no advertising networks, and no third-party tracking scripts of any kind. The only browser storage Vault uses is localStorage — a standard browser feature that stores data locally on your device and is never transmitted automatically.

06
Data sharing and third parties

We do not share data with third parties because we do not hold any of your data. The only external services Vault interacts with are:

Google APIs — only when you explicitly connect Google Drive backup. This interaction is governed by Google's own Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

Google Fonts — Vault loads the DM Serif Display and DM Sans typefaces from Google Fonts. This is a standard web font request and does not involve any personal data from Vault.

07
Encryption

Vault offers optional AES-256-GCM encryption for backup files. When enabled, your data is encrypted entirely within the browser before being written to any backup destination. The encryption key is derived from your chosen password and never leaves your device.

When you set a password, a recovery key file is downloaded automatically. This is the only way to recover your data if you forget your password — Vault holds no record of your password or encryption key and cannot recover your data on your behalf.

08
Children's privacy

Vault is a general-purpose financial tracking tool and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under the age of 13.

09

If this policy changes in a material way, the effective date at the top of this page will be updated. As Vault has no account system, we are unable to notify users directly — we encourage you to review this page occasionally.

01
What is Vault?

Vault is an independent, single-file web application for tracking personal savings. It runs entirely in your browser with no server, no account, and no subscription. It is available at vaultsavings.app.

Vault is built for people who want a clear picture of their savings without handing their financial data to a third party. Everything you enter stays on your device unless you explicitly connect a backup of your choosing.

02
Open source & independent

Vault is an independent project with no corporate entity behind it. It is not affiliated with any financial institution, investment platform, or data company.

The app is a single HTML file — you can save it locally, inspect every line, and run it offline. There are no build steps, no frameworks, and no hidden dependencies.

03
Currency data

When exchange rates are needed — for example, when converting transaction amounts between currencies — Vault fetches daily rates from ExchangeRate-API. Rates are cached in your backup file so that subsequent devices and sessions use the stored data rather than making additional requests.

Attribution: Exchange rate data is provided by ExchangeRate-API. Rates are indicative midpoint rates updated daily and are intended for informational purposes only.
