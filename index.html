Setting Up Google Drive Sync

1
Sign in with your Google account
  1. Make sure you're signed into Vault with the Google account you want to use for sync — this is done on the login screen.
  2. Vault uses your Google identity to securely access a private file in your own Google Drive.
💡 Vault only requests permission to manage files it creates itself (drive.file scope). It cannot see or access any other files in your Drive.
2
Connect Google Drive
  1. Open Menu → Connect to Google Drive.
  2. Click 🔗 Connect Google Drive.
  3. A Google permissions popup will appear — click Allow to grant Vault access to create and manage its sync file.
  4. Vault will immediately create a file called vault-data.json in your Drive and upload your current data.
⚠️ If you see a warning that the app isn't verified, click Advanced → Go to Vault (unsafe). This is expected for personal apps not submitted to Google's verification programme.
3
Sync across devices
  1. Once connected, Vault automatically saves every change to your Drive file.
  2. On any other device, open Vault, sign in with the same Google account, and connect Google Drive — Vault will find the existing file and load your data.
  3. Changes sync in the background whenever you switch back to the app.
✅ Your data is stored as vault-data.json in your Google Drive. You can view it at drive.google.com — but don't edit it manually or it may break the sync.
